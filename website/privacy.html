OTPGetr — Privacy Policy

Effective 17 July 2026

OTPGetr is a personal desktop application that reads one-time verification codes from your Gmail and copies them to your clipboard. This policy explains exactly what it accesses and where that data goes. In plain terms: your email stays on your machine, except for the narrow image case described below.

What we access

  • With your permission, OTPGetr connects to your Gmail through Google's official API and reads only your most recent messages, solely to locate a verification / one-time code.
  • It only ever reads mail to find a code. It does not send, delete, label, or modify any message.

How your data is used

  • Local first. Text-based codes are detected on your own computer using pattern matching. That content never leaves your device.
  • Image codes (the one exception). Some senders put the code inside an image. To read those, OTPGetr sends that image (downscaled) to the Anthropic API (Claude) purely to extract the code text. Only the image needed to read the code is sent, and only when a code cannot be read locally.
  • Extracted codes are placed on your clipboard so you can paste them. Nothing else is done with your mail.
We never sell, rent, or share your email data or codes for advertising, profiling, or any purpose beyond delivering the code to you. Google user data is not used to train any general-purpose AI model.

What is stored, and where

  • Your Google sign-in token is stored only on your own device (a local token.json) so you don't log in every time.
  • A local activity log and the date your token was granted are kept on your device to run the app. None of this is uploaded to us.
  • We operate no servers and keep no database of your information. There is nothing for us to retain.

Third-party processing

The only third party that can receive any of your Gmail content is Anthropic, and only the specific image described above, only to read the code. See Anthropic's terms and privacy at anthropic.com/legal/privacy.

Google Limited Use

OTPGetr's use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Your control

  • Revoke access at any time from Google Account → Permissions.
  • Deleting the app and its local token.json removes all stored access.
