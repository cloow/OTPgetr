OTPGetr

A small desktop helper that grabs your one-time verification codes.

What it does

OTPGetr runs quietly on your own Windows PC. When a verification code (OTP) arrives in your Gmail, it copies the code to your clipboard and shows a small pop-up so you can paste it — without opening Gmail. That's the whole app.

How it uses your Gmail

  • Reads only. It uses Google's official Gmail API only to read your recent messages. It can never send, delete, or change your mail.
  • Only to find codes. It looks at your most recent messages to extract the verification code, nothing more.
  • Mostly on your device. Plain-text codes are found locally on your PC. Only when a code is inside an image is that image sent out to be read (see our Privacy Policy).
  • You stay in control. Revoke access anytime at Google Account permissions.

Who it's for

It's a personal utility for individuals who want their login codes handy. It isn't a commercial service and doesn't have accounts, ads, or analytics.
