OTPgetr

A personal Windows desktop app for one-time verification codes.

The purpose of OTPgetr is to save you the hassle of hunting through your inbox for login codes. It is a small desktop application that runs on your own Windows PC and uses the Google Gmail API to read the one-time verification codes (OTPs) that arrive in your Gmail, then copies the code to your clipboard and shows a small pop-up so you can paste it into whatever you are signing in to — all without opening Gmail yourself.

What the app is for

Many websites and apps email you a short numeric code to confirm it's really you. Normally you have to switch to Gmail, open the newest message, read the code, and type it back in. OTPgetr does that step for you:

  • It watches for the newest verification-code email in your Gmail.
  • It extracts the one-time code from that message.
  • It copies the code to your clipboard and shows a small pop-up.
  • You paste the code and finish signing in — faster, with no inbox digging.

That is the entire purpose of the app. It is a personal convenience utility — not a commercial service. It has no user accounts, no ads, and no analytics.

Why it needs access to your Gmail

Reading the verification code is the app's purpose, so it needs permission to read your recent Gmail messages through Google's official Gmail API. Here is exactly how that access is used:

  • Read-only. OTPgetr requests a read-only Gmail scope. It can never send, delete, label, or modify any of your mail.
  • Only to find codes. It looks at your most recent messages solely to locate and extract the one-time verification code. It does not store, share, or use your email for any other purpose.
  • Mostly on your own device. Plain-text codes are detected locally on your PC. Only when a code appears inside an image is that image sent out to be read (details in our Privacy Policy).
  • You stay in control. You can revoke OTPgetr's access at any time from your Google Account permissions page.

Who it's for

OTPgetr is a personal utility for individuals who frequently receive login codes by email and want them handy without switching to their inbox every time. It is installed and run by the user on their own computer.
